Sous Vide Chicken Cordon Bleu

Description

Sous vide is a cooking technique that heats food with water. Here's how it works. Food is placed in a vacuum-sealed bag and cooked in a temperature-regulated water bath.

The food cooks evenly because it is surrounded by water, not air. Because the water doesn't go above a certain temperature, you never have to worry about overcooked food

In this recipe we'll teach you how to make an amazing and easy Chicken Cordon Bleu with just some minor preparation, that tastes amazing every single time

Since sous vide is so simple, you can make a lot of them ahead, and being them already cooked and vacuum-sealed, you can either store them up to 7 days in your fridge, or up to 3 months in the freezer!

Ingredients

  • Whole chicken breast
  • Asiago cheese, or a similar hard texture italian cheese
  • Ham slices
  • Salt

Optional

  • Bread crumbs for breading
  • Black pepper
  • Red chili pepper flakes

Steps

  1. De-bone and butterfly your chicken breasts
  2. Season with salt (and optional black pepper) what will be on the inside of the cordon bleu
  3. Lay one slice of ham followed by one slice of Asiago cheese
  4. Close the chicken breast enclosing all the stuffing on the inside
  5. Season the external part of the chicken
  6. Bag the chicken in a vacuum-sealed bag (beware of it being sous vide proof!)
  7. Cook at 65Â°c for 2 hours

Now that the time is up, your chicken cordon bleu are ready!

You can either finish them now or leave them for later

Finishing the dish

You can bread the cordon bleu and fry them up in some oil, or deep fry them

Or you could also spare some extra calories and just grill them up in a bbq grill or a skillet
